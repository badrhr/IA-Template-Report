This chapter presents the results obtained by applying the methodology described 
in Chapter~\ref{ch:method}. The findings are arranged in a logical sequence, 
progressing from overall algorithm performance to data-driven findings, and 
concluding with the ablation study. Each result is directly linked to a specific 
objective or research question stated in Chapter~\ref{ch:into}.

\noindent The results chapter tells a reader about your findings based on the 
methodology you have used to solve the investigated problem. For example:
\begin{itemize}
    \item If your project aims to implement an algorithm for its analysis, the
    results may be the performance of the algorithm obtained using a relevant
    experiment design.

    \item If your project aims to solve some problems/research questions over a
    collected dataset, the results may be the findings obtained using the applied
    tools/algorithms/etc.
\end{itemize}

\section{How to Present Your Results}
\label{sec:how-to-present}
% ================================================================

Arrange your results and findings in a logical sequence. Use a bottom-up approach
by first presenting raw or processed outputs, then progressively building toward
interpretation. Each result should directly correspond to an objective or research
question stated in your introduction.

When presenting your results, keep the following principles in mind:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Be objective:} Report what you observed without over-interpreting.
    Save deeper analysis for the Discussion chapter.

    \item \textbf{Be precise:} Use exact numerical values, units, and statistical
    measures (e.g., accuracy, F1-score, RMSE) where applicable.

    \item \textbf{Be complete:} Report both positive and negative findings.
    Unexpected or poor results are equally important and should not be omitted.

    \item \textbf{Be consistent:} Use the same notation and terminology throughout
    to ensure clarity.
\end{itemize}
